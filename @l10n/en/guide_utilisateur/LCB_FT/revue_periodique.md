# Periodic review

## Periodic review (Remediation)

**Periodic review**, also called *remediation*, is the process of **regularly updating** investor files (distributors, service providers, etc.).  
It ensures that collected information and documents remain **compliant with regulatory requirements** and **AML/CFT obligations**.

On the platform, the **next review date** is visible directly in the **investor file**.

## How the review date is calculated

The **next review date** is automatically determined based on two criteria:

1. The **file approval date** (first approval or most recent approval).  
2. The **AML risk level** assigned to the file.

ðŸ‘‰ **The higher the risk, the more frequent the review.**

---

## Default review intervals

| Risk level | Review frequency |
|------------------|--------------------|
| Low           | Every **3 years** |
| Medium            | Every **2 years** |
| High / High +  | Every **1 year**  |

{% admonition type="info" %}
These settings are **configurable**: each client can customize review intervals directly in their environment **settings**.
{% /admonition %}

In addition to automatic calculation, the platform provides a **manual option** to **enter or modify the approval date directly**.
